Black pepper beef.

Disclaimer:your taste buds are about to be aroused


Ingredients.

  • 2 large whole black peppercorns
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon red chili flakes
  • 1 teaspoon masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 2 cups fresh breadcrumbs
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro, for garnish

    • How to prepare the meal.

    1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
    2. Toast the black peppercorns in a dry pan over medium heat for 2-3 minutes, until fragrant and lightly charred.
    3. Transfer the toasted black peppercorns to a food processor and add the garlic, olive oil, red chili flakes, garam masala, cayenne pepper, turmeric, coriander powder, ginger powder, cumin powder, and fennel seeds.
    4. Pulse the spice blend until it forms a coarse paste.
    5. Gradually stir in the salt and the whole milk. The mixture should be slightly thicker than the consistency of pancake batter.
    6. Divide the mixture into 2 equal portions. Roll each portion into a log approximately 8 inches (20 cm) long.
    7. Place the breadcrumbs in a shallow bowl. Dredge the rolled pepper logs in the breadcrumbs, ensuring that the entire surface of the logs is evenly coated.
    8. Arrange the coated logs, seam-side down, on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
    9. Bake the logs for 15-20 minutes, until the outside is golden brown and crispy.
    10. Transfer the logs to a cutting board and slice them into 1-inch (2.5 cm) thick slices using a sharp knife.
    11. To serve, arrange the sliced pepper logs on a platter. Drizzle each slice with a bit of olive oil and garnish with a sprinkle of chopped fresh cilantro.
